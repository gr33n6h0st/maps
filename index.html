LENS 01 // SPATIAL ANTAGONISM

The Blind Curve

Hanover Public School suffers from geometric antagonism. The street layout features a sharp curve that actively hides small pedestrians.

LOG: THE HANOVER GEOMETRY
Analysis of the sightlines suggests a driver has less than 2 seconds to react to a child emerging from behind winter snow windrows. This geometric failure renders crossing guards the only line of defense in a layout where driver sightlines are reduced by 40%.
ANALYSIS: THE IMPACT ZONELOC: HANOVER RD
LENS 02 // SPATIAL ANTAGONISM

The Squeeze

At Lester B. Pearson, the 'Kiss and Ride' infrastructure collapses under vehicle volume, prioritizing machine movement over child safety.

LOG: THE DOUBLE-LANE TRAP
Observed 50 cars: 38 children exited into the traffic 'drive-through' lane; only 12 utilized the curb. 6-foot snow windrows force drivers to 'nose out' blindly into the pedestrian path.

LENS 03 // ENV. DEPRIVATION

The Urban Void

The 150 Howden Blvd site represents a distinct form of environmental deprivation, signaling that this community space is now 'dead'.

LOG: THE ARCHITECTURE OF NEGLECT
The removal of the indoor playground has created a 200-meter 'hole' in the social fabric. This 'void' offers 0 'eyes on the street,' resulting in a 100% loss of passive surveillance.
LENS 04 // ENV. DEPRIVATION

The Arterial Wall

The high-speed commuter traffic on Dixie Road creates an arterial wall that severs the school zone from residential neighborhoods.

LOG: MACHINE SPACE
Children are often trapped in the median for 15-20 seconds as the light cycles, surrounded by 60km/h+ transit, creating an environment of extreme sensory hostility.
