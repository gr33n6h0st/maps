LENS 01 // SPATIAL ANTAGONISM

The Blind Curve

Hanover Public School features a geometric anomaly: a sharp, blind curve immediately preceding the crosswalk. This represents a failure of street design where geometry actively obscures children from driver view.

OBSERVATION LOG [Feb 12]:
Drivers approaching Hanover maintain speeds of 40-44km/h. The curve geometry naturally restricts visibility to approx 25m. When combined with the reaction time formula, a driver requires 26m to stop. The road design creates a "Death Zone" where physics makes mechanical stopping impossible before impact.

LENS 02 // SPATIAL ANTAGONISM

The Squeeze

At Lester B. Pearson Catholic School, the "Kiss & Ride" infrastructure has collapsed. The single-lane entry forces a "Double Lane Trap," and snow mounds at the exit create a complete visual blockage.

OBSERVATION LOG [Feb 12]:
The designated drop-off lane is designed for 12 cars but held 45 during observation (300% Capacity). At the parking lot exit, plow trucks leave 6ft snow mounds. Parents must "nose out" blindly into the sidewalk path, prioritizing car movement over student safety.

LENS 03 // ENVIRONMENTAL DEPRIVATION

The Urban Void

150 Howden Blvd is a vast open field where the Rec Centre previously stood. This void now acts as a disconnected gap between the neighborhood "catwalks" and the school entrance.

OBSERVATION LOG [Feb 12]:
Students arrive via narrow catwalks that dump them into this unlit, open field. Unlike a residential street which has "eyes on the street" (windows facing the path), this route creates a tunnel effect. In winter mornings (7:30 AM), students walk through this 200m stretch in total darkness.

LENS 04 // ENVIRONMENTAL DEPRIVATION

The Arterial Wall

Dixie Road functions as a "River of Steel." The 60km/h speed limit and 6-lane width create a sensory and physical barrier that severs the school from the eastern neighborhood.

OBSERVATION LOG [Feb 12]:
Noise levels average 75dB, requiring shouting for communication. The crosswalk signal allows 25 seconds for crossing 6 lanes (approx 35 meters). For a small child (walking speed 1.0m/s), this requires 35 seconds. The infrastructure prioritizes car flow over child capability.
