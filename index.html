Lens 01 // Spatial Antagonism

The Blind Curve

The approach to Hanover Public School is defined by a sharp, blind curve that restricts driver sightlines to 25 meters. Physics dictates that at 44km/h, a vehicle requires 26.4 meters to stop. This is a mechanical conflict: the road geometry renders a driver incapable of stopping for a child they cannot see.

OBSERVATION LOG: FEB 12
Observed average vehicle approach speed: 44km/h. Physical measurements confirm visibility is restricted to 25 meters by residential fencing and street curvature. Mathematical stopping distance (26.4m) exceeds physical sightline.

Lens 02 // Spatial Antagonism

The Squeeze

The "Kiss & Ride" at Lester B. Pearson is operating at 375% capacity. Designed for 12 cars, the facility manages 45+ vehicles during peak surges. This overcrowding, combined with 5ft snow mounds at the exit, forces parents to "blind nose" into the student crosswalk.

OBSERVATION LOG: PEAK CONGESTION
Queue count peaked at 45 cars. System overflow forces illegal idling on the main road. Snow mounds at the exit measure over 5 feet high, obstructing the view of the sidewalk entirely.

The Urban Void

The Arterial Wall

Dixie Road is a 6-lane physical barrier where signal timing is calculated for adult walking speeds. A student requires 35 seconds to cross, but is only granted 25 seconds of protected signal time, leaving them in the road for 10 seconds of unprotected traffic flow.
