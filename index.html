Field Note Summary: The Architecture of Neglect

This project utilizes digital ethnography to map the "Home Range" of Brampton, Ontario. Specifically, it analyzes how the high-speed arterial design of Dixie Road conflicts with the child-centric infrastructure of Lester B. Pearson and Hanover Public Schools. The following 4 lenses reveal a landscape where safety is secondary to speed.

01. The Blind Curve

MOBILITY
GEOMETRY OF RISK
Winter snow mounds and road curvature reduce driver visibility to <15m.

02. The Squeeze

INFRASTRUCTURE
CAPACITY FAILURE
Kiss & Ride designed for 12 cars. Actual volume: 45 cars. Queue spills to arterial road.

03. The Urban Void

DEPRIVATION
DEFENSIVE ARCHITECTURE
Concrete barriers block the demolished Rec Centre, creating an unsupervised "dead zone."

04. The Arterial Wall

ANTAGONISM
MACHINE SPACE
6 Lanes of traffic vs. 1 School Zone. Noise and pollution buffer isolates the school.
