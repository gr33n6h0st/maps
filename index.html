LENS 01 // SPATIAL ANTAGONISM

The Blind Curve

Hanover Public School features a geometric anomaly: a sharp, blind curve immediately preceding the crosswalk. This represents a failure of street design where geometry actively obscures children from driver view.

OBSERVATION LOG [Feb 12]:
Drivers approaching Hanover maintain speeds of 40-44km/h despite the "School Zone" signage. The curve geometry naturally restricts visibility to approx 25m. When combined with the reaction time formula, a driver at this speed requires 26m to stop safely. The road design creates a "Death Zone" where physics makes mechanical stopping impossible before impact.

LENS 02 // INFRASTRUCTURE FAILURE

The Squeeze

At Lester B. Pearson Catholic School, the "Kiss & Ride" infrastructure has collapsed. The single-lane entry forces a "Double Lane Trap," and snow mounds at the exit create a complete visual blockage.

OBSERVATION LOG [Feb 12]:
The designated drop-off lane is designed for 12 cars but held 45 during observation (300% Capacity). At the parking lot exit, plow trucks leave 6ft snow mounds. Parents exiting the lot must "nose out" blindly into the sidewalk path to see traffic, prioritizing car movement over student safety. Lane width measured at 4.8m.

LENS 03 // ENVIRONMENTAL DEPRIVATION

The Urban Void

150 Howden Blvd represents "Environmental Deprivation." The demolition of the Community Rec Centre has removed "eyes on the street" (Jacobs, 1961), leaving a defensive vacuum defined by concrete barriers.

OBSERVATION LOG [Feb 12]:
The site is blocked by concrete Jersey barriers. The removal of the building eliminated the 'safe haven' effect. During the 1km walk to school, this 200m stretch offers zero shelter, lighting, or adult presence. It effectively creates a hole in the social fabric of the neighborhood.

LENS 04 // ENVIRONMENTAL DEPRIVATION

The Arterial Wall

Dixie Road functions as a "River of Steel." The 60km/h speed limit and 6-lane width create a sensory and physical barrier that severs the school from the eastern neighborhood.

OBSERVATION LOG [Feb 12]:
Noise levels average 75dB, requiring shouting for communication. The crosswalk signal allows 25 seconds for crossing 6 lanes (approx 35 meters). For a small child (walking speed 1.0m/s), this requires 35 seconds. The infrastructure prioritizes car flow over child capability.
